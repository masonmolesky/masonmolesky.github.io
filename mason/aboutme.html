MASON MOLESKY

Mason Molesky is a cybersecurity and national security professional who currently works as a senior consultant at Guidehouse. He has many years of expertise at numerous different government agencies.


Most recently, he served as the Special Assistant to the DoD Deputy Chief Information Officer for Cybersecurity DCIO(CS) / DoD Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and as the head of the Cybersecurity International and Industry Engagement Offices. In this role, Mason helped shape the strategic direction of the DoD’s cybersecurity; develop robust cybersecurity risk management methodologies, practices, and metrics; and lead DoD-wide strategic cybersecurity initiatives to burn down cyber risk enabling and protecting the warfighter. He also oversaw and led the DoD CIO’s Cybersecurity Partnerships divisions working to strengthen partnerships with international and industry partners.


Prior to this position, Mason developed National-level strategy and policy for General Paul Nakasone, the Director of the National Security Agency/Commander of US Cyber Command; Marianne Bailey, the National Manager for National Security Systems; Jeanette Manfra, the Assistant Secretary for Cybersecurity at the Department of Homeland Security; and Steven Hernandez, Chief Information Security Officer at the Office of Inspector General, Department of Health and Human Services.


Mason is also a PhD candidate in computer science at The George Washington University. He holds a master’s degree in cybersecurity from The George Washington University and a bachelor’s degree in mathematics and computer science from Alma College. His work has been nationally and internationally published winning several awards including, most recently, the 2020 MBAA International Distinguished Research Paper Award from the Society for the Advancement of Information Systems. In 2018, Mason became an ARCS Scholar and in 2016 became a CyberCorps: Scholarship for Service recipient.


Outside of work, Mason teaches cybersecurity master’s courses at The George Washington University and sings in the National Philharmonic Chorale. He is an avid hiker and enjoys spending time with family and friends.
